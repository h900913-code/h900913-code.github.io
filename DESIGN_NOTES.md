# Design Notes

## Why these references

Public view-count data for personal academic homepages is generally not public. Instead, I used award-winning or widely referenced academic personal sites as a proxy for "proven" design quality and clarity.

## Parallel review tracks

- Track 1: local code and information-architecture audit
- Track 2: external reference scan for academic personal-homepage patterns

## Reference cases

### 1. The Academic Designer, Best Personal Academic Websites 2025

https://theacademicdesigner.com/2025/winners-of-the-best-personal-academic-websites-contest-2025/

Patterns extracted:

- clear first-screen identity
- visually separated sections instead of plain CV stacking
- stronger hierarchy between overview and detail

### 2. Madeline Eppley

https://www.madeline-eppley.com/

Patterns extracted:

- warm first-person welcome
- immediate statement of research focus
- approachable landing page instead of a document dump

### 3. Erika Cedillo

https://erikacedillo.com/

Patterns extracted:

- strong headline with role framing
- card-based sectioning
- human tone plus professional credibility

### 4. Hira Javed

https://hirajaved.com/

Patterns extracted:

- narrative structure
- image-led layout
- strong typography and generous spacing

## Design principles chosen for this site

- Put the research identity above the CV chronology.
- Make the homepage feel like an introduction, not a resume printout.
- Use stronger visual grouping: hero, stat cards, panels, timelines, and card lists.
- Improve click paths to key destinations: research, publications, projects, contact.
- Keep the bilingual structure, but give it a more intentional visual language.

## Applied changes

- Rebuilt the homepage into a hero-led entry point with CTA buttons and focus keywords.
- Reframed research pages into topic/method cards.
- Strengthened publications and projects with clearer intros and card styling.
- Turned contact pages into action-oriented contact panels.
- Moved the visual system away from a flat white academic template into layered surfaces, stronger typography, and softer background depth.

## Future follow-ups

- Add external academic profile links such as Google Scholar / ORCID when available.
- Add selected-paper links or downloadable CV actions.
- Add page-level lead images or subtle diagrams if the site grows beyond the current scope.
